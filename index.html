Dashboard 1 - Sales Overview

Case Study: Advanced Sales Performance Analysis (QoQ). This Power BI Dashboard was designed for executive decision-making, integrating data from multiple sources to track 9.9M Total Profit from 20M Total Sales, achieving 49.9% profitability. The data analysis highlighted significant performance variance across regions and a sharp sales drop in 2016 following a 2015 peak. I utilized DAX functions to create dynamic time-intelligence calculations (like Quarter-over-Quarter) and implemented interactive data visualization techniques to allow managers to instantly filter sales trends, identify low-performing states, and strategize for future growth. This comprehensive Sales Analysis Dashboard turns raw transaction data into clear, actionable insights.

Dashboard 2 - Employees Overview

Case Study: HR Workforce Analysis and Headcount Planning. This Power BI Dashboard provides a detailed overview of the organization's Human Resources data, covering 689 employees and a total salary of 1.4M. The data analysis focuses on departmental distribution (Manufacturing is largest), gender ratios (mostly male, 65%), and geographical distribution (Main center has the largest headcount of 251). I employed advanced filtering and data visualization to highlight the peak headcount period in 2019, enabling HR leaders to better understand employee trends, plan recruitment strategies, and manage compensation and workforce efficiency effectively.

Dashboard 3 - Sales & COGS

Case Study: Retail Profitability and Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Analysis. This Power BI Dashboard offers a focused view on retail financial health, tracking 15.38K Total Sales and an average COGS of 307.59. The core of this financial data analysis is understanding profitability drivers, showing stable sales across key cities (Yangon, Naypyitaw, Mandalay) and analyzing payment distribution (Cash is the main payment at 34.4%). Through strategic data visualization, the dashboard allows stakeholders to analyze gender-based purchasing patterns (F52%/M48%) and identify opportunities to reduce COGS and maximize profit margins.
